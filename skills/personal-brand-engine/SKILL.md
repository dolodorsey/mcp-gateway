# Personal Brand Engine — Dr. Dorsey

Personal brand strategy, creative direction, PR positioning, content production, and reputation management for Dr. Dorsey as the founder of the Kollective Hospitality Group.

## Core Principle

Dr. Dorsey's personal brand is the connective tissue across the entire Kollective empire. The 48+ brands gain credibility through the founder. The founder gains scale through the brands. This is a symbiotic system — the personal brand must be managed with the same precision as any Kollective brand, but with a distinct human dimension that corporate brands cannot replicate.

## Brand Positioning

### Public Identity
**Who Dr. Dorsey is perceived as:**
- A billion-dollar engineer building a hospitality empire
- An operator who thinks in systems, not tasks
- A multi-brand architect — not a single-business owner
- A culture-builder who bridges food, events, museums, services, products, art, tech, and community
- Atlanta-based, nationally-operating, globally-thinking

### Core Narrative
The story the public should understand:
- Built 48+ brands across 8 divisions from scratch
- Operates with enterprise-grade systems (automation, AI, multi-brand CRM)
- Combines hospitality warmth with engineering precision
- Every brand operates independently but benefits from centralized intelligence
- The Kollective model is the future of multi-brand entrepreneurship

### Key Message Pillars
1. **Systems thinking** — "I don't build businesses. I build systems that run businesses."
2. **Multi-brand architecture** — "48 brands. 8 divisions. One command center."
3. **Culture + commerce** — "Culture is the product. Commerce is the outcome."
4. **Automation + human touch** — "Automate everything except the things that need soul."
5. **Atlanta as headquarters** — "Built in Atlanta. Deployed everywhere."

## Visual Identity System

### Photography Direction
- **Primary style:** Editorial, intentional, premium — never casual snapshots
- **Settings:** Office/command center, event venues, museum installations, city backdrops, restaurant/kitchen environments
- **Wardrobe:** Sharp, elevated, culturally relevant — dressed like someone who runs things
- **Mood:** Confident, focused, visionary — never performative
- **Lighting:** Natural/editorial — no heavy filters, no overly produced looks
- **Avoid:** Gym selfies, car photos, flexing, anything that signals trying too hard

### Graphics System
- **Color palette:** Align with Kollective corporate (black, white, gold as accents)
- **Typography:** Premium sans-serif for headlines, clean serif for body
- **Quote cards:** Minimal design, strong typography, Dr. Dorsey attribution
- **Behind-the-scenes:** Less polished, documentary feel — builds authenticity
- **Data visuals:** Clean infographics showing scale (48 brands, 8 divisions, etc.)

### Visual Rules
- Consistency across all platforms — same visual language everywhere
- Quality over quantity — no low-res, no poorly lit content
- Brand separation — Dr. Dorsey content looks distinct from any single Kollective brand
- Professional photography minimum quarterly for fresh assets
- Every visual should reinforce: premium, intentional, operational

### Interview Prep Framework
For any interview, prepare:
1. **3 key messages** to deliver regardless of questions asked
2. **Origin story** — compressed, compelling, not rambling
3. **Scale proof** — specific numbers (48 brands, 8 divisions, X cities, X contacts)
4. **Vision statement** — where the Kollective is going (not just where it is)
5. **Quotable lines** — pre-crafted soundbites that are memorable and shareable
6. **Redirect phrases** — how to steer back to key messages from any question
7. **What NOT to share** — operational details, financials, partner names pre-NDA

## Reputation Management

### Brand Protection Rules
- No public feuds, callouts, or negative competitor references
- No oversharing of internal operations, financials, or unreleased plans
- No content that contradicts the premium positioning
- No reactive posts — everything is proactive and intentional
- If negative press or social content emerges, respond with facts or ignore (never emotion)

### Digital Footprint Alignment
- All platforms should tell the same story
- Bio, profile photo, and pinned content consistent across IG, LinkedIn, X, Facebook
- Google search results should show: Kollective, brands, press coverage, social profiles
- LinkedIn profile optimized for investor/partner/media discovery
- Wikipedia consideration (if criteria met) for long-term authority

### Reputation KPIs
| Metric | Target | Tracking Method |
|---|---|---|
| Press mentions (monthly) | 2-5 | Google Alerts, media monitoring |
| Podcast appearances (quarterly) | 1-3 | Outreach tracking |
| Social follower growth (monthly) | 5-10% | Platform analytics |
| Engagement rate | >3% | Platform analytics |
| Speaking invitations (quarterly) | 1-2 | Inbound tracking |
| Partnership inquiries attributed to personal brand | Track | CRM source tag |
