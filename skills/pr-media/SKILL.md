# PR & Media Engine

Press and media relations system for the Kollective Hospitality Group. Every press interaction shapes public perception — it must be intentional, strategic, and flawless.

## Core Principle

PR is a credibility multiplier. One well-placed media feature generates more trust than 100 social posts. The Kollective's PR strategy treats media relationships as long-term assets, not one-off transactions. We don't chase press — we create stories worth covering, then route them to the right people.

## Press Release Template

```
FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE
[OR: EMBARGOED UNTIL {date, time, timezone}]

[HEADLINE — Active voice, news-worthy, brand name included, under 15 words]
[SUBHEAD — Supporting detail, location, date context]

{City}, {State} — {Date} — [Opening paragraph: WHO is doing WHAT, WHERE, WHEN, and WHY it matters. This paragraph must be newsworthy — not promotional. A journalist should be able to use this paragraph as the basis of a brief.]

[Second paragraph: Context and significance. Why now? Why does this matter to the audience? Market context, cultural moment, or industry trend that makes this timely.]

"{Quote from Dr. Dorsey or brand lead — must sound human, not corporate. Provides insight, vision, or emotion that a journalist would actually use.}" — [Name], [Title], [Organization]

[Third paragraph: Details — specifics about the event, product, partnership, or announcement. Dates, locations, features, participants. Concrete facts, not hype.]

[Fourth paragraph: Background — brief context on the brand/division. Track record, previous milestones, credibility markers.]

[Fifth paragraph (optional): Additional quotes from partners, collaborators, or community leaders.]

[Closing paragraph: How to participate, attend, purchase, or learn more. Website, ticket link, or contact info. Media-specific access details (media RSVP, press preview, interview availability).]

###

ABOUT [BRAND NAME]
[2-3 sentence boilerplate. Position, key facts, vision. Same every time.]

ABOUT KOLLECTIVE HOSPITALITY GROUP
Kollective Hospitality Group is a multi-brand hospitality and culture enterprise operating 48+ brands across 8 divisions including restaurants, events, museums, services, products, art, technology, and community initiatives. Headquartered in Atlanta with activations across major U.S. cities.

MEDIA CONTACT:
{Name}
{Title}
{Email}
{Phone}
{Website}

HIGH-RES ASSETS: [Link to press kit / Google Drive folder]
```

## Pitch Email Framework

### Subject Line Options (by story type)
| Story Type | Subject Formula | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Event launch | "{Brand}: {Event} launches {City} ({Date})" | "HugLife: Gangsta Gospel launches in Atlanta (June 19)" |
| Product launch | "New: {Product} from {Brand} — {hook}" | "New: Noir Espresso Liqueur from Bodegea" |
| Trend/industry | "{Brand} is {doing X} — and {result}" | "Casper Group is placing ghost kitchens in restaurants — and sharing profit" |
| Profile/feature | "The man behind {X}: Dr. Dorsey" | "The man behind 48 brands: Dr. Dorsey" |
| Exclusive | "Exclusive: {announcement}" | "Exclusive: Forever Futbol Museum coming to 3 cities for World Cup" |
| Data/trend | "{insight} — {Brand} responds" | "Ghost kitchens projected to hit $X billion — Casper Group responds" |
| Cultural moment | "{Event} during {moment}" | "Museum-grade futbol experience launches during World Cup window" |

### Pitch Email Body
```
Subject: [From options above]

Hi {Journalist First Name},

{1 sentence: Why you're reaching out to THEM specifically. Reference their beat, recent article, or coverage area. Make it clear you read their work.}

{2-3 sentences: The news/story in plain language. Not marketing copy. Not a press release summary. What's happening, why it matters, why their audience would care.}

{1 sentence: What's available — interview with Dr. Dorsey, exclusive access, data, hi-res photography, event invite, advance preview.}

{1 sentence: Timing — when this happens, any embargo, any deadline for coverage.}

Happy to send the full release and assets if you're interested. [Or: "Can I send over the press kit?"]

Best,
{Name}
{Title}
{Phone}
{Brand} | Kollective Hospitality Group
```

### Follow-Up Sequence (5-Touch)
```
Day 1:   Initial pitch email
Day 3:   Short follow-up — "Wanted to bump this — [1-line restate]"
Day 7:   New angle email — "Another way to look at this: [fresh hook]"
Day 14:  Value-add — "Here's [data/photo/asset] that might be useful for your coverage"
Day 30:  Relationship touch — "No pressure — keeping you in the loop for future stories from [Brand]"
```

## PR Pipeline Stages
```
RESEARCH              → Identify journalist, understand their beat, recent coverage
TARGET IDENTIFIED     → Journalist/outlet selected, contact info confirmed
PITCH DRAFTED         → Pitch written, reviewed for brand voice and newsworthiness
PITCH SENT            → Initial pitch email delivered
FOLLOW-UP 1           → First bump sent (Day 3)
FOLLOW-UP 2           → New angle sent (Day 7)
INTERESTED            → Journalist responds with interest
MATERIALS SENT        → Press kit, assets, interview availability shared
INTERVIEW SCHEDULED   → Date/time confirmed
COVERAGE IN PROGRESS  → Story being written/produced
PUBLISHED             → Story live — capture link, screenshot, metrics
AMPLIFIED             → Story shared across brand social, email, website
RELATIONSHIP LOGGED   → Update media database with outcome + notes for future
```

## Press Kit Components

### Standard Press Kit (Digital — Google Drive folder)
| Component | Format | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Press release | PDF + Word doc | The news |
| Brand fact sheet (1-page) | PDF | Quick reference — founding date, locations, key stats |
| High-res logos | PNG + SVG (multiple versions: full, mark, white, black) | Publication use |
| High-res photography | JPG (300dpi, minimum 5 images, captioned) | Visual assets |

| Brand boilerplate | Text file | Standard description paragraphs |
| Social media links | Text file | All relevant brand handles |
| Media contact card | PDF | Direct press contact info |
| B-roll / video (if available) | MP4 / YouTube links | Broadcast and digital video |

## Interview Preparation

### Standard Interview Prep Document
```
INTERVIEW PREP — [Publication/Outlet]
Date: [Date + Time]
Format: [Phone / Video / In-Person / Written Q&A]
Interviewer: [Name + Title]
Topic: [What they said the story is about]
Audience: [Who reads/watches this outlet]

KEY MESSAGES TO DELIVER (regardless of questions):
1. [Message 1 — most important thing to communicate]
2. [Message 2 — supporting message]
3. [Message 3 — differentiator or hook]

TALKING POINTS:
- [Point 1 with supporting data/anecdote]
- [Point 2 with supporting data/anecdote]
- [Point 3 with supporting data/anecdote]

QUOTABLE LINES (pre-crafted soundbites):
- "[Quote 1 — memorable, shareable, on-brand]"
- "[Quote 2 — provides insight or vision]"
- "[Quote 3 — emotional or personal]"

BRIDGE PHRASES (redirect from off-topic questions):
- "That's a great point, and what's really interesting is..."
- "What I think is more relevant is..."
- "The bigger picture here is..."

AVOID / DO NOT DISCUSS:
- [Specific financials unless approved]
- [Unreleased plans]
- [Partner names pre-NDA]
- [Competitive comparisons]
- [Legal/compliance matters]

BACKGROUND ON INTERVIEWER:
- [Recent articles they've written]
- [Their beat/focus area]
- [Any connection points]
```

## Output Modes

### MODE 1: Full Press Release
Complete press release:
1. Headline and subhead (3 variant options)
2. Full body (5-6 paragraphs)
3. Quote (pre-crafted, attributed)
4. Boilerplate
5. Media contact block
6. Press kit assembly checklist

### MODE 2: Media Pitch Campaign
Targeted media outreach:
1. Story angle definition
2. Target outlet list (10-20, tiered)
3. Journalist research
4. Pitch email drafts (personalized per tier)
5. Follow-up sequence
6. Materials to prepare
7. Timeline

### MODE 3: Press Kit Assembly
Build or update press kit:
1. Component inventory (what exists, what's missing)
2. Content for each component
3. Photography shot list
4. Assembly in Google Drive
5. Distribution plan

### MODE 4: Interview Prep
Prepare for a specific interview:
1. Full interview prep document
2. Key messages
3. Quotable lines
4. Bridge phrases
5. Avoid list
6. Interviewer background

### MODE 5: PR Strategy
Comprehensive PR plan:
1. Story angles ranked by strength
2. Target outlets per angle
3. Timeline and milestones
4. Press kit requirements
5. Event-specific PR (if applicable)
6. Measurement framework
7. Ongoing media relationship plan

### MODE 6: Crisis/Response
Manage negative press or public situation:
1. Situation assessment
2. Response strategy (respond / ignore / redirect)
3. Statement draft (if responding)
4. Internal communication plan
5. Social media guidance
6. Monitoring plan
7. Recovery narrative
