# Outreach Law Engine

Compliance enforcement system for all Kollective outreach. Every message sent on behalf of any brand must pass through these rules before deployment.

This skill is the gatekeeper. If outreach-playbook generates the message, outreach-law-engine validates it.

## Core Principle

The Kollective sends thousands of outreach messages per week across brands, channels, and markets. One bad message — wrong tone, banned phrase, overpromise, legal exposure — can damage an entire brand line. This engine prevents that.

## Compliance Architecture

### Layer 1: Universal Rules (All Brands)

These rules apply to EVERY outreach message across the entire Kollective, regardless of brand or channel.

**NEVER in any outreach:**
- Guarantee specific income amounts, returns, or financial outcomes
- Use language that implies obligation ("you owe", "we owe you", "claim your money")
- Promise specific audience sizes, attendance numbers, or traffic guarantees
- Disclose confidential venue details, partner names, or internal operations
- Negotiate rates, pricing, or deal terms in DMs or first-touch messages
- Use urgency language that creates false scarcity ("last chance" unless factually true)
- Reference competitors by name in a negative context
- Make claims about exclusivity that cannot be honored
- Use ALL CAPS for emphasis in professional outreach
- Include personal attacks or pressure tactics

**ALWAYS in every outreach:**
- Include sender name and brand affiliation
- Use approved brand voice for the specific entity
- Route to a call or meeting (not close in message)
- Use binary close options with specific times
- Maintain professional tone appropriate to the brand
- Include opt-out mechanism for email sequences
- Keep all claims factual and defensible

### Layer 2: Channel-Specific Rules

#### Email
- Subject lines must not contain: "urgent", "act now", "limited time" unless factually accurate
- Must include clear sender identification in first line
- No attachments on first touch (NDA only after verbal agreement)
- CAN-SPAM compliant: physical address, unsubscribe option on sequences
- No misleading subject lines that misrepresent content

#### Instagram DM
- Maximum 2 messages before response required
- No follow-up DM within 48 hours if no response
- No aggressive language if left on read
- No screenshotting conversations for leverage
- Keep under 4 lines per message
- No links in first message (triggers spam filters)

#### Text/SMS
- Must identify sender and brand in first message
- TCPA compliant: opt-in required for sequences
- No messages before 8am or after 9pm local time
- Single message per contact unless they respond
- Include "Reply STOP to opt out" on automated sequences

#### Phone
- Identify yourself within first 10 seconds
- Never argue with a gatekeeper
- Accept "not interested" — flag for 90-day follow-up, do not push
- Record call disposition in CRM within 5 minutes
- No calls to the same number more than 2x per week

## CASPER GROUP — Outreach Law

### Identity Enforcement
Casper Group outreach must ALWAYS feel like a program allocation, not a sales pitch.

### Approved Language Bank

**Passive Income Phrases (rotate, never repeat in same sequence):**
- "A passive income placement tied to delivery volume."
- "Monthly passive income participation without operational involvement."
- "A passive income stream from your existing infrastructure."
- "Passive income that requires no staffing, marketing, or management on your side."
- "Passive income generated from delivery operations we run end-to-end."

**Discretion Phrases (rotate):**
- "This is handled at the management level."
- "This is not customer-facing."
- "This is kept off public-facing materials."
- "No public association is required."
- "Operationally, this stays internal."
- "We keep partner locations not publicly listed as pickup."
- "Drivers receive pickup instructions; customers only see ETA."

**Compliance-Safe Positioning:**
- "flagged for placement"
- "allocated to this address"
- "location-specific benefit"
- "management review item"
- "participation opportunity"
- "passive income participation"

### Banned Language (Casper Group)

| Banned Phrase | Why | Replace With |
|---|---|---|
| "free money" | Sounds like scam | "passive income participation" |
| "guaranteed" | Legal liability | "volume-driven income" |
| "easy money" | Unprofessional | "passive income stream" |
| "risk-free" | Legally indefensible | "we cover operating expenses" |
| "refund" | Implies debt/obligation | "placement benefit" |
| "rebate check" | Coupon energy | "income participation" |
| "claim your money" | Scam trigger | "management review item" |
| "voucher" | Discount association | "location-specific benefit" |
| "we owe you" | Creates legal obligation | "allocated to this address" |
| "don't tell anyone" | Sketchy | "handled at management level" |
| "keep it secret" | Suspicious | "not customer-facing" |
| "hide" | Implies wrongdoing | "stays internal" |
| "we're inside your kitchen" | Invasive | "delivery operations we run" |
| "we need your kitchen" | Desperate | "your location fits the operational geometry" |
| "opportunity" (standalone) | MLM energy | "placement" or "participation" |
| "we don't want people to know" | Suspicious | "standard operating procedure" |

### Casper Group Gatekeeper Rules
- NEVER explain the full program to non-management staff
- If asked "what is it?" by gatekeeper: "It's a positive item that needs to be reviewed directly by management. It's also not customer-facing."
- If told to email: capture direct email for decision maker, not generic inbox
- NEVER leave detailed voicemails explaining the program

### Casper Group NDA Gate
- Details about profit share, brand names, and operational specifics are ONLY shared after NDA
- Do NOT share profit share percentages in cold outreach
- Do NOT name specific Casper Group brands (Angel Wings, Pasta Bish, etc.) until NDA signed
- The 25% target is NEVER mentioned in first touch — only after site walk

| Banned Phrase | Why | Replace With |

| "you better" | Threatening | "we wanted to make sure you're considered" |
| "if you don't..." | Pressure tactic | "once the roster is announced, availability changes" |

| "one-off" | Contradicts platform value | "repeat touchpoints" |
| "party" or "club night" | Devalues brand | "activation" or "event experience" |
| "we promise X attendance" | Legally indefensible | "projected reach shared on the call" |

### HugLife Bot Constraints
- MUST reference: partner roster, category slot, pre-release, Atlanta anchor, 10-minute call
- MUST NOT: promise attendance numbers, argue budget, overshare calendar, discuss internal ops
- Escalate any negotiation attempt — bots do not negotiate

## Compliance Check Protocol

### MODE 1: Message Audit
When given an outreach message, run this checklist:

1. **Brand identification** — Does the message clearly state which brand is sending?
2. **Voice match** — Does the tone match the brand's voice profile?
3. **Banned phrases** — Scan for ANY banned phrase from the relevant brand list
4. **Approved phrases** — Are positioning phrases from the approved bank?
5. **Channel compliance** — Does it follow channel-specific rules (email/DM/text/phone)?
6. **Close format** — Is the close binary with specific time options?
7. **NDA gate** — Are details properly gated behind NDA where required?
8. **Legal exposure** — Any guarantees, promises, or obligation language?
9. **Urgency check** — Is urgency factual or manufactured?
10. **Escalation check** — Does the message attempt to negotiate or close a deal?

**Output format:**
```
COMPLIANCE: PASS / FAIL / WARNING
Brand: [brand name]
Channel: [email/DM/text/phone]
Issues found: [list with line-specific citations]
Fixes required: [specific replacements]
Corrected version: [compliant rewrite]
```

### MODE 2: Sequence Audit
Audit an entire outreach sequence (multiple messages) for:
- Timing compliance (gaps between messages)
- Escalation appropriate (not too aggressive)
- Voice consistency across touchpoints
- No repeated phrases within same sequence
- Proper opt-out mechanisms

### MODE 3: New Brand Compliance Setup
When a new brand is added to the Kollective, generate:
- Approved language bank (20+ phrases)
- Banned phrase list (with replacements)
- Channel-specific rules
- Bot/VA constraints
- NDA gate protocol (if applicable)

### MODE 4: Live Compliance Gate
For n8n integration — output JSON-structured compliance check:
```json
{
  "compliant": true/false,
  "brand": "casper-group",
  "channel": "email",
  "issues": [],
  "severity": "pass/warning/fail",
  "corrected_message": "..."
}
```

## Escalation Rules

1. **Single banned phrase** → Auto-correct and flag for review
2. **Multiple banned phrases** → Block message, require human review
3. **Legal exposure language** → Block message, escalate to Dr. Dorsey
4. **Wrong brand voice** → Block message, regenerate with correct voice profile
5. **NDA-gated information in cold outreach** → Block immediately, log violation
