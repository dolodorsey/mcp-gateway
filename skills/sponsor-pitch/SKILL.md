# Sponsor Pitch Engine

Sponsorship revenue architecture and packaging system for all Kollective event and cultural properties. This skill designs the buyable property — turning events, museums, and activations into structured, tiered, revenue-generating partnership opportunities.

## Core Principle

We are not asking for sponsorship. We are offering category placement inside premium platforms. The language, the packaging, and the pitch must all communicate: "This is a slot that will be filled — the question is whether it's filled by you or your competitor."

## Positioning Rule

Never say "sponsor." Say "partner," "placement," or "slot." Never say "sponsorship package." Say "category placement" or "partner tier." Never say "we need sponsors." Say "we're finalizing the partner roster."

The sponsor should feel like they're being invited into something — not pitched at.

## Sponsor Tier Structure

### Tier 1: Title Partner ($$$)
**What they get:**
- Category exclusivity across ALL events in property
- Naming integration ("Paparazzi presented by [Brand]")
- Logo on all marketing materials (flyers, social, email, signage)
- On-site activation space (booth, sampling, lounge)
- Content package (professional photos + video featuring brand)
- Social media integration (tagged in all event posts)
- Email feature to subscriber list
- VIP access for brand team
- First right of renewal for next year/cycle
- Custom activation design consultation

**What it costs:** Highest tier — property-dependent, typically $25K-100K+

### Tier 2: Category Partner ($$)
**What they get:**
- Category exclusivity for specific events (not full property)
- Logo on event-specific marketing materials
- On-site presence (product placement, sampling)
- Content package (photos + select video)
- Social media mentions (2-3 per event)
- VIP access (limited)

**What it costs:** Mid-tier — typically $10K-40K per event or $25K-75K per tour

### Tier 3: Supporting Partner ($)
**What they get:**
- Logo on event signage (not all marketing)
- On-site product presence
- Social media mention (1 per event)
- Event access for brand team

**What it costs:** Entry tier — typically $5K-15K per event

### Tier 4: In-Kind / Product Partner
**What they get:**
- On-site product placement
- Social media mention
- Photo documentation

**What they provide:** Product supply for events (no cash) — e.g., a water brand provides free water for all attendees.

## What The Sponsor Is Actually Buying

This is the internal framing — know what you're selling even if you don't say it this way:

1. **Access to audience** — Concentrated, curated, engaged audience in premium environment
2. **Category protection** — Their competitor cannot be there
3. **Content generation** — Professional photos/video of their product in a cool environment
4. **Repeat impressions** — Tour footprint means multiple cities, multiple events
5. **Cultural credibility** — Association with premium cultural platform (not a generic event)
6. **Sampling opportunity** — Put product directly in hands of target demographic
7. **Social proof** — Tagged in content that reaches beyond attendees
8. **B2B signal** — Being listed as a partner of a cultural property has downstream value

## Pricing Logic

### Factors That Determine Price
| Factor | Impact on Price |
|---|---|
| Property prestige | Higher for museum-grade, lower for single events |
| Audience size (projected) | Larger audience = higher value |
| Number of cities/events | Multi-city tour = premium multiplier |
| Category scarcity | High-demand categories (spirits, energy) cost more |
| Exclusivity level | Full property exclusivity = highest premium |
| Timing (World Cup window, holidays) | Cultural moment multiplier |
| Activation complexity | Custom integration costs more |
| Content package scope | More content = more value |

### Pricing Framework
```
Base price = Audience value × category demand × event count
Exclusivity premium = Base × 1.5-2x for full property lock
Tour premium = Per-event × 0.8 per additional event (volume discount)
Custom activation = Base + build-out cost + management fee
```

## Objection Handling

**"Send the deck."**
"Absolutely. We're pre-release right now, so we match partners to the right slot on a quick 10-minute call first, then send the deck right after."

**"What's the audience size?"**
"We'll share projections by city and event on the call. The value here is: concentrated audience + multi-city + category protection + premium environment — not just a number."

**"We already sponsor events."**
"Perfect — that means you understand the value of activation. This is a platform play with repeat touchpoints and controlled integration, not a one-night logo placement."

**"What do you need from us?"**
"Depends on the slot — cash, product, hybrid, or a combination. The point is: we lock your category, integrate you cleanly, and you get repeated exposure in a brand-safe environment."

**"That's expensive."**
"What's the alternative? Buying the same audience through paid ads costs 3-5x more with no exclusivity, no activation, and no content. This is a locked position in a premium environment."

**"We need to think about it."**
"Totally understand. Just know we're finalizing the roster — once a category is filled, it's locked. I'd hate for you to lose the slot because of timing."

## Close Language (Rotate)
- "Let's reserve the category slot while we finalize terms."
- "We'll hold the slot and lock it with a simple agreement."
- "If you want category protection, we should lock it before the roster is announced."
- "Once the partner roster is public, pricing and availability change."
